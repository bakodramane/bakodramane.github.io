## 🇬🇧 English

This article is part of weekly updates on new developments in the use of AI methods and tools of surveys (households, individuals, farms…) and administrative data for official statistics

Prepared by: Dramane Bako, Statistician/Data scientist (FAO Statistics Division, Rome, Italy)

Coverage Period: 08–14 September 2025

Key words: AI, administrative data, machine learning, data quality, data analysis

Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive overview of the latest AI-powered tools and methods transforming the landscape of data editing, cleaning, processing, analysis, reporting, and dissemination. As of September 2025, the field is characterized by a rapid shift towards more intelligent, automated, and accessible solutions, driven by advancements in natural language processing (NLP), machine learning (ML), and cloud computing. For researchers and statistical offices, these innovations offer unprecedented opportunities to enhance data quality, accelerate analysis, and derive deeper insights from complex datasets.

Key trends identified in this report include the rise of conversational AI interfaces that democratize data analysis, the integration of automated pattern recognition for proactive data quality management, and the widespread adoption of cloud-native architectures that enable real-time collaboration and scalability. The market is also witnessing a convergence of capabilities, with many platforms now offering end-to-end solutions that span the entire data lifecycle, from ingestion and cleaning to visualization and reporting.

This report is structured to provide a detailed analysis of the current tool ecosystem, categorized by functionality, followed by an in-depth examination of key technology trends, vendor landscapes, and pricing models. It concludes with specific recommendations tailored to the unique needs of statistical offices, research institutions, and smaller research teams, along with a forward-looking perspective on the future of AI in data-centric professions.

1. Introduction: The AI-Driven Transformation of Data Workflows

The traditional paradigms of data management and analysis are undergoing a profound transformation, catalyzed by the increasing sophistication and accessibility of artificial intelligence. For decades, data professionals have relied on a combination of manual processes, rule-based systems, and statistical software to navigate the complexities of data editing, cleaning, and analysis. While effective, these methods have often been characterized by their labor-intensive nature, steep learning curves, and limited capacity to handle the sheer volume and velocity of modern data.

Today, a new generation of AI-powered tools is emerging to address these challenges, offering a more intelligent, automated, and intuitive approach to data workflows. These tools leverage cutting-edge techniques in machine learning, natural language processing, and computer vision to automate repetitive tasks, uncover hidden patterns, and empower users with more powerful and accessible analytical capabilities. From AI-driven data cleaning platforms that can automatically identify and correct errors to conversational AI chatbots that can generate complex analyses from simple natural language queries, the impact of AI is being felt across every stage of the data lifecycle.

This report aims to provide a comprehensive and up-to-date overview of this rapidly evolving landscape, with a specific focus on the tools and methods that are most relevant to the work of researchers and statistical offices. By understanding the capabilities and limitations of these new technologies, data professionals can make more informed decisions about how to best leverage AI to enhance their work, improve the quality of their data, and unlock new frontiers of discovery.

2. AI-Powered Data Editing and Cleaning Tools

The foundation of any robust analysis lies in the quality of the underlying data. Historically, data editing and cleaning have been among the most time-consuming and manual aspects of the research process, often accounting for up to 80% of a data professional's time. The advent of AI is rapidly changing this paradigm, with a new generation of tools designed to automate and enhance the process of data quality management.

These tools leverage a variety of AI techniques, including machine learning, statistical analysis, and pattern recognition, to identify and correct a wide range of data issues, from simple formatting errors to complex, context-dependent anomalies. By automating these tasks, AI-powered data cleaning tools not only save valuable time and resources but also improve the accuracy and consistency of the data, leading to more reliable and trustworthy analytical outcomes.

This section provides a detailed overview of the leading AI-powered data editing and cleaning tools available in 2025, categorized by their primary focus and target audience.

2.1. Enterprise-Grade Data Observability and Quality Platforms

For large organizations and statistical offices, data quality is not just a technical issue but a critical component of data governance and compliance. Enterprise-grade data observability platforms are designed to provide a holistic and proactive approach to data quality management, offering end-to-end visibility across complex data ecosystems.

Monte Carlo stands out as a leader in this category, offering a comprehensive data observability platform that helps organizations achieve more reliable and trustworthy data. By leveraging machine learning, Monte Carlo can automatically monitor data pipelines, identify anomalies, and provide actionable insights to resolve data quality issues before they impact downstream analyses. The platform's key features include automated data profiling, anomaly detection, and root cause analysis, all of which are designed to reduce the time to detection and resolution of data incidents. As noted in a recent industry review, Monte Carlo has been recognized as the #1 Data Observability Platform by G2 for seven consecutive quarters, a testament to its market leadership and customer satisfaction [1].

Alteryx Designer, another prominent player in the enterprise space, provides a powerful and intuitive platform for end-to-end data preparation and analytics. Its code-free, drag-and-drop interface makes it accessible to a wide range of users, from data analysts to business users. Alteryx's AI and machine learning capabilities are integrated throughout the platform, enabling users to automate complex data cleaning and transformation tasks, as well as build and deploy predictive models. The platform's ability to connect to a wide range of data sources, including cloud platforms like Snowflake and Databricks, makes it a versatile solution for organizations with diverse data ecosystems [2].

2.2. Open Source and Accessible Data Cleaning Solutions

While enterprise platforms offer powerful capabilities, their cost and complexity can be prohibitive for smaller research teams, academics, and individual researchers. Fortunately, a growing number of open-source and accessible tools are available to address the needs of this community.

OpenRefine, originally developed by Google, is a powerful and popular open-source tool for data cleaning and transformation. With over 15,500 monthly downloads and more than 800 academic citations in 2023, OpenRefine has established itself as a staple in the data professional's toolkit [3]. The tool's intuitive, point-and-click interface allows users to easily explore, clean, and transform messy data, while its support for a wide range of file formats and APIs makes it a versatile solution for a variety of data cleaning tasks. OpenRefine's clustering algorithms are particularly effective for identifying and correcting inconsistencies in categorical data, a common challenge in survey and administrative data.

DataWrangler, another open-source tool, offers an interactive and visual approach to data cleaning. Developed by the Stanford Visualization Group, DataWrangler allows users to clean and reshape data by simply demonstrating the desired transformations on a small sample of the data. The tool then automatically infers the corresponding data transformation rules and applies them to the entire dataset. This interactive and intuitive approach makes DataWrangler an excellent tool for both novice and experienced users who want to quickly and easily clean their data without writing any code.

2.3. Specialized AI-Powered Data Cleaning Applications

In addition to general-purpose data cleaning platforms, a growing number of specialized tools are emerging to address specific data quality challenges and use cases.

Numerous AI is a prime example of this trend, offering a powerful AI-powered tool specifically designed for cleaning and enriching data within spreadsheets. By integrating directly with Google Sheets and Microsoft Excel, Numerous AI allows users to perform a wide range of data cleaning tasks, from bulk cleaning and data normalization to sentiment analysis and text categorization, all within their familiar spreadsheet environment. This targeted approach makes Numerous AI an ideal solution for content marketers, researchers, and e-commerce businesses who work extensively with unstructured and semi-structured data in spreadsheets [4].

Trifacta, now part of the Alteryx family, has long been recognized for its innovative approach to data preparation. The platform's AI-powered engine can automatically detect patterns and anomalies in the data and suggest intelligent transformations to clean and structure the data. This predictive transformation capability, combined with a visual and interactive interface, makes Trifacta a powerful tool for accelerating the data preparation process and improving data quality.

3. AI-Powered Data Processing and Analysis Tools

Once data has been cleaned and prepared, the next critical step is to extract meaningful insights through processing and analysis. This is another area where AI is making a significant impact, with a new generation of tools designed to automate complex analytical tasks, uncover hidden patterns, and make advanced analytics more accessible to a broader range of users. These tools leverage a variety of AI techniques, including machine learning, natural language processing, and deep learning, to provide capabilities that were once the exclusive domain of expert data scientists.

This section explores the landscape of AI-powered data processing and analysis tools, highlighting the key platforms and technologies that are shaping the future of data analysis for researchers and statistical offices.

3.1. Comprehensive Analytics and Business Intelligence Platforms

Comprehensive analytics platforms are designed to provide an end-to-end solution for data analysis, from data integration and preparation to visualization and reporting. These platforms are increasingly incorporating AI and machine learning capabilities to automate and enhance every step of the analytical workflow.

Domo is a cloud-native platform that offers a comprehensive suite of tools for data integration, analysis, and visualization. Its AI service layer, which includes features like predictive analytics, natural language queries, and automated data storytelling, is designed to make data analysis more intelligent and accessible. As noted in a recent review, Domo's AI capabilities are woven throughout the platform, from its AI-enhanced data exploration tools to its intelligent chat interface that allows users to ask questions of their data in plain English [5].

Microsoft Power BI, a leader in the business intelligence market, has also made significant investments in AI. Its integration with Azure Machine Learning allows users to build and deploy sophisticated predictive models directly within their Power BI dashboards. The platform also includes a range of native AI capabilities, such as anomaly detection, time-series forecasting, and key influencers analysis, which help users uncover deeper insights from their data. For organizations already invested in the Microsoft ecosystem, Power BI offers a seamless and powerful solution for AI-driven analytics [6].

Tableau, now part of Salesforce, is another major player in the analytics space that is embracing AI. With the introduction of Tableau GPT and Tableau Pulse, the platform is leveraging generative AI to make data analysis more conversational and intuitive. These features allow users to interact with their data using natural language, automatically generate insights and visualizations, and receive proactive alerts about important changes in their data. Tableau's deep integration with Salesforce also makes it a powerful tool for organizations that want to combine their operational and analytical data [7].

3.2. Specialized and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Tools

In addition to comprehensive analytics platforms, a growing number of specialized tools are emerging to address specific analytical needs, particularly in the area of exploratory data analysis (EDA). These tools are designed to help users quickly and easily explore new datasets, identify patterns and relationships, and generate initial hypotheses.

Powerdrill Bloom is a new entrant in this space that is specifically designed for AI-powered exploratory data analysis. As highlighted in a recent article, Powerdrill Bloom allows users to interact with their data using natural language, and the tool automatically generates relevant visualizations and statistical summaries. Its multimodal capabilities, which support text, voice, and image inputs, make it a particularly innovative and user-friendly tool for EDA [8].

IBM Watsonx is another powerful platform that is well-suited for exploratory data analysis. Its emphasis on enterprise governance and its ability to handle large and complex datasets make it a good choice for statistical offices and other organizations with stringent data security and compliance requirements. Watsonx's conversational AI capabilities allow users to interact with their data in a natural and intuitive way, while its integration with a wide range of data sources and analytical tools provides a flexible and extensible platform for EDA [8].

3.3. The Rise of AI Chatbots for Data Analysis

The increasing sophistication of large language models (LLMs) has led to the emergence of a new category of AI-powered data analysis tools: AI chatbots. These tools allow users to perform complex analytical tasks by simply having a conversation with an AI assistant.

ChatGPT, powered by OpenAI's GPT models, has demonstrated impressive capabilities in this area. By leveraging its ability to understand natural language and generate code, ChatGPT can help users with a wide range of data analysis tasks, from data cleaning and transformation to statistical modeling and visualization. While not a specialized data analysis tool, its versatility and accessibility make it a valuable resource for researchers and data professionals.

Claude, developed by Anthropic, is another powerful LLM that has shown promise for data analysis. Its large context window, which allows it to process and analyze large amounts of text at once, makes it particularly well-suited for tasks that involve summarizing and synthesizing information from multiple sources.

4. AI-Powered Data Reporting and Dissemination Tools

The final stage of the data lifecycle, reporting and dissemination, is where insights are communicated to stakeholders and the value of the data is ultimately realized. AI is also transforming this stage, with a new generation of tools designed to automate the creation of reports, dashboards, and visualizations, and to make data-driven storytelling more engaging and impactful.

These tools leverage AI to not only automate the process of report generation but also to enhance the clarity and relevance of the information being presented. By using natural language generation (NLG), for example, these tools can automatically generate narrative summaries of key findings, making it easier for non-technical audiences to understand the implications of the data. Similarly, by using machine learning to identify the most relevant and impactful visualizations, these tools can help ensure that the data is presented in the most effective way possible.

This section provides an overview of the leading AI-powered data reporting and dissemination tools, categorized by their primary focus and capabilities.

4.1. AI-Powered Reporting and Dashboarding Platforms

Whatagraph is a marketing intelligence platform that has made significant investments in AI. As detailed in a recent review, Whatagraph's AI-powered features include an AI chatbot that can answer natural language questions about the data, as well as an AI report summary feature that can automatically generate narrative summaries of key findings. These features, combined with the platform's strong focus on data integration and visualization, make it a powerful tool for marketing agencies and other organizations that need to create and distribute regular reports to clients and stakeholders [9].

Klipfolio is another leading platform in this space, offering a comprehensive suite of tools for building and sharing real-time business dashboards. Klipfolio's AI-powered features include the ability to ask questions of the data in natural language, as well as a range of pre-built visualizations and templates that make it easy to create professional-looking dashboards. The platform's strong focus on data integration, with support for over 130 different data sources, makes it a versatile solution for a wide range of reporting and dashboarding needs [9].

4.2. AI-Powered Data Visualization Tools

Data visualization is a critical component of effective data communication, and AI is playing an increasingly important role in this area. AI-powered data visualization tools can help users automatically identify the most effective chart types for their data, as well as generate interactive and engaging visualizations that allow users to explore the data in more depth.

ThoughtSpot is a search-driven analytics platform that has been a pioneer in the use of AI for data visualization. The platform's natural language interface allows users to simply type in their questions, and ThoughtSpot will automatically generate the most appropriate visualizations to answer those questions. This search-driven approach makes data visualization more accessible to a broader range of users, and helps to break down the barriers between data and decision-makers [10].

Domo, in addition to its broader analytics capabilities, also offers a range of AI-powered data visualization tools. As highlighted in a recent article, Domo's AI tools can provide predictive analytics, recommend visualizations, and automate data ingestion. The platform's support for a wide range of chart types and its ability to create interactive and drillable dashboards make it a powerful tool for creating engaging and informative data visualizations [6].

4.3. AI-Powered Data Storytelling

Data storytelling is the art of communicating insights from data in a compelling and narrative-driven way. AI is now being used to automate and enhance this process, with a new generation of tools that can automatically generate data-driven stories and presentations.

Juice Analytics is a company that has long been focused on the art and science of data storytelling. Their platform, Juicebox, allows users to create interactive data stories that combine data visualizations with narrative text and other multimedia elements. While not a fully automated AI tool, Juicebox provides a powerful framework for creating engaging and impactful data stories [11].

They advocate for a new generation of AI-powered dashboards that can not only present data but also explain why metrics are changing and what to do next. This vision of AI-powered data storytelling, which combines data visualization with automated insights and recommendations, represents the future of data communication [12].

The landscape of AI-powered tools for data professionals is evolving at an unprecedented pace. Résumé exécutif

Ce rapport offre un aperçu complet des derniers outils et méthodes basés sur l'IA qui transforment le paysage de l'édition, du nettoyage, du traitement, de l'analyse, de la création de rapports et de la diffusion des données. Le marché est également témoin d'une convergence des capacités, de nombreuses plateformes offrant désormais des solutions de bout en bout qui couvrent l'ensemble du cycle de vie des données, de l'ingestion et du nettoyage à la visualisation et à la création de rapports.

Ce rapport est structuré pour fournir une analyse détaillée de l'écosystème actuel des outils, classés par fonctionnalité, suivie d'un examen approfondi des principales tendances technologiques, des paysages des fournisseurs et des modèles de tarification. Il se termine par des recommandations spécifiques adaptées aux besoins uniques des offices statistiques, des instituts de recherche et des petites équipes de recherche, ainsi que par une perspective prospective sur l'avenir de l'IA dans les professions axées sur les données.

1. Introduction : La transformation des flux de travail de données par l'IA

Les paradigmes traditionnels de la gestion et de l'analyse des données subissent une profonde transformation, catalysée par la sophistication et l'accessibilité croissantes de l'intelligence artificielle. Pendant des décennies, les professionnels des données se sont appuyés sur une combinaison de processus manuels, de systèmes basés sur des règles et de logiciels statistiques pour naviguer dans les complexités de l'édition, du nettoyage et de l'analyse des données. Bien qu'efficaces, ces méthodes ont souvent été caractérisées par leur nature laborieuse, leurs courbes d'apprentissage abruptes et leur capacité limitée à gérer le volume et la vitesse des données modernes.

Aujourd'hui, une nouvelle génération d'outils basés sur l'IA émerge pour relever ces défis, offrant une approche plus intelligente, automatisée et intuitive des flux de travail de données. Ces outils exploitent des techniques de pointe en apprentissage automatique, en traitement du langage naturel et en vision par ordinateur pour automatiser les tâches répétitives, découvrir des modèles cachés et doter les utilisateurs de capacités analytiques plus puissantes et accessibles. Des plateformes de nettoyage de données basées sur l'IA qui peuvent identifier et corriger automatiquement les erreurs aux chatbots d'IA conversationnels qui peuvent générer des analyses complexes à partir de simples requêtes en langage naturel, l'impact de l'IA se fait sentir à chaque étape du cycle de vie des données.

Ce rapport vise à fournir un aperçu complet et à jour de ce paysage en évolution rapide, en mettant l'accent sur les outils et les méthodes les plus pertinents pour le travail des chercheurs et des offices statistiques. En comprenant les capacités et les limites de ces nouvelles technologies, les professionnels des données peuvent prendre des décisions plus éclairées sur la meilleure façon d'exploiter l'IA pour améliorer leur travail, la qualité de leurs données et ouvrir de nouvelles frontières de découverte.

2. Outils d'édition et de nettoyage de données basés sur l'IA

La base de toute analyse robuste réside dans la qualité des données sous-jacentes. Historiquement, l'édition et le nettoyage des données ont été parmi les aspects les plus chronophages et manuels du processus de recherche, représentant souvent jusqu'à 80 % du temps d'un professionnel des données. L'avènement de l'IA change rapidement ce paradigme, avec une nouvelle génération d'outils conçus pour automatiser et améliorer le processus de gestion de la qualité des données.

Ces outils exploitent une variété de techniques d'IA, y compris l'apprentissage automatique, l'analyse statistique et la reconnaissance de motifs, pour identifier et corriger un large éventail de problèmes de données, des simples erreurs de formatage aux anomalies complexes et dépendantes du contexte. En automatisant ces tâches, les outils de nettoyage de données basés sur l'IA permettent non seulement d'économiser un temps et des ressources précieux, mais aussi d'améliorer la précision et la cohérence des données, ce qui conduit à des résultats analytiques plus fiables et dignes de confiance.

Cette section fournit un aperçu détaillé des principaux outils d'édition et de nettoyage de données basés sur l'IA disponibles en 2025, classés par leur objectif principal et leur public cible.

2.1. Plateformes d'observabilité et de qualité des données de niveau entreprise

Pour les grandes organisations et les offices statistiques, la qualité des données n'est pas seulement un problème technique, mais un élément essentiel de la gouvernance et de la conformité des données. Les plateformes d'observabilité des données de niveau entreprise sont conçues pour fournir une approche holistique et proactive de la gestion de la qualité des données, offrant une visibilité de bout en bout sur des écosystèmes de données complexes.

Monte Carlo se distingue comme un leader dans cette catégorie, offrant une plateforme complète d'observabilité des données qui aide les organisations à obtenir des données plus fiables et dignes de confiance. En exploitant l'apprentissage automatique, Monte Carlo peut surveiller automatiquement les pipelines de données, identifier les anomalies et fournir des informations exploitables pour résoudre les problèmes de qualité des données avant qu'ils n'affectent les analyses en aval. Les principales fonctionnalités de la plateforme comprennent le profilage automatisé des données, la détection des anomalies et l'analyse des causes profondes, toutes conçues pour réduire le temps de détection et de résolution des incidents de données. Comme indiqué dans une récente revue de l'industrie, Monte Carlo a été reconnu comme la plateforme d'observabilité des données n°1 par G2 pendant sept trimestres consécutifs, un témoignage de son leadership sur le marché et de la satisfaction de ses clients [1].

Alteryx Designer, un autre acteur majeur dans le domaine des entreprises, fournit une plateforme puissante et intuitive pour la préparation et l'analyse des données de bout en bout. Son interface sans code, par glisser-déposer, la rend accessible à un large éventail d'utilisateurs, des analystes de données aux utilisateurs professionnels. Les capacités d'IA et d'apprentissage automatique d'Alteryx sont intégrées dans toute la plateforme, permettant aux utilisateurs d'automatiser des tâches complexes de nettoyage et de transformation des données, ainsi que de construire et de déployer des modèles prédictifs. La capacité de la plateforme à se connecter à un large éventail de sources de données, y compris des plateformes cloud comme Snowflake et Databricks, en fait une solution polyvalente pour les organisations avec des écosystèmes de données diversifiés [2].

2.2. Solutions de nettoyage de données open source et accessibles

Bien que les plateformes d'entreprise offrent des capacités puissantes, leur coût et leur complexité peuvent être prohibitifs pour les petites équipes de recherche, les universitaires et les chercheurs individuels. Heureusement, un nombre croissant d'outils open source et accessibles sont disponibles pour répondre aux besoins de cette communauté.

OpenRefine, développé à l'origine par Google, est un outil open source puissant et populaire pour le nettoyage et la transformation des données. Avec plus de 15 500 téléchargements mensuels et plus de 800 citations académiques en 2023, OpenRefine s'est imposé comme un incontournable dans la boîte à outils du professionnel des données [3]. L'interface intuitive de l'outil, par pointer-cliquer, permet aux utilisateurs d'explorer, de nettoyer et de transformer facilement des données désordonnées, tandis que son support pour un large éventail de formats de fichiers et d'API en fait une solution polyvalente pour une variété de tâches de nettoyage de données. Les algorithmes de clustering d'OpenRefine sont particulièrement efficaces pour identifier et corriger les incohérences dans les données catégorielles, un défi courant dans les données d'enquête et administratives.
